// SCENE 1

My fists flew at him and recoiled like springs. Bloodied, bruised and tired my knuckles were, but I didn't stop. I couldn't stop. The red sloshes of the impact haunted my ears every punch I landed. Frostbite gnawed at me, only worsened by the physical trauma I was enduring. I felt a crack in his jaw, then around his eye, and finally in one of his cheekbones. I twitched at the disgust I felt.

The sound of my punches echoed down the tunnels, though not far. I couldn't tell if the cave was just narrow, or if it had no exit and no entry. The cave rumbled and cracked with every impact, as if it was about to collapse and kill us both. Perhaps it would have been better that way. The hiss in my throat every time I breathed was the only thing I could hear clearly.

I winced in pain. He deserves this. If it wasn't for him, it would be someone else. It felt like I was striking needles, or the cave's ice itself. *Or a shattered mirror.*

He wasn't even protecting bracing himself. He hadn't been for a while. He laid there limp as one can be, but I didn't stop. I couldn't. I kept gasping, yet I couldn't breathe. I don't know if it was the depth of the crystalline cave, or something else taking my air away. The jagged reflections in the ice mocked me. Stalactites fell and shattered behind me.

My lungs felt easy, and my throat wasn't closed off. My eyes were sharp, and my heart beat relaxed, yet I could not help but choke.

Exhausted, my pace slowed before stopping. His body laid there like a corpse. Disfigured, limp and unconscious. "What? Have you finally had enough?" I asked him, wiping my bloody hands on myself. "You arrogant asshole."

Suffocating on the lack of air, I stood myself up just barely. The pressure caused my ears to start ringing like a church's bell. I barely felt any sensation on my skin, and my muscles worked against me, but I knew I wouldn't have to go far. It was right there.

The glow of the crystal I was sent here for was dimmed by all of the ice between us, but nothing would be enough to dull its power. I covered my eyes, barely holding my arm on top of my face. It was calling me. Its deep, rich hum worked against the ringing in my head, like it was trying to calm me. It knew of my presence. For a moment, I even admired that nothing else was loud.

A cold prickle of paranoia forced me to look back. I needed to make sure he was still there, slumped and broken on the dark cave floor where I’d left him. I turned my head toward the shadows.

The ice was empty.

Before my brain could even process the blank space, a heavy, freezing weight clamped onto my shoulder. I spun around. He was right there, looming over me. His amalgamated face stared directly into mine, and my heart skipped a beat. I covered my face and closed my eyes.

Then I felt his hand on my shoulder. I turned around. I saw his amalgamated face staring at mine for a moment, and my heart skipped a beat. I covered my face and closed my eyes.

The world stood still. The vibration of the crystal's hum rattled my jaw. I grinded my teeth together, wishing it all wasn't real, but it wasn't in the first place. Never was.

Then the cold vanished.

// SCENE 2

I gasped for air, opening my eyes to my "home". The lantern on the table in front of me was losing its brightness. When I had sat down, the oil canister was full. I tugged on my rub burned wrists. The ropes were still strong.

The room around me was a void. I could not tell if I was "dreaming". I could finally breathe again, but all I smelled was the char of a recently extinguished candle.

I let another moment of silence pass, only perturbed by the ticking of a clock I did not have. It ticked several times in succession, then paused. I saw practically nothing beyond the table, my eyes still adjusting from the brightness of the crystal. But that should not have been possible.

Then, a moment later, I hear a breath escape in front of me. He was preying like an animal.

Two faint cyan lights opened across the table, with an almond shaped void in the midst of them. The crystal-like glow is as mesmerizing as ever. A chair scrapes at the floor as he scoots into the light. His face now fully visible, beared the same deformations I had given him earlier. But I knew it wouldn't last. It was merely an act of catharsis he allowed me to have once in a while.

The floorboards creaked unnaturally. The sound echoed like we were in a hall in one moment, then sounded as clear as anything the next. One moment I heard an owl from the outside, and in the next I heard songbirds chirp. His attempts at deception were cheap.

"I know what you're thinking," he told me. A moment of silence passed as I attempted to ignore him before returning to my actual mission, the cave. I didn't care about anything he could have possibly had to tell me. The crystal is all I needed. I swallowed, but my mouth was dry.

"You need something to drink?" he asked me. I stared him dead in the eyes. He knew what I thought, I knew what he thought. Why would we ever need to talk was beyond me.

"Why do you reject me? We're a team. We're one person. I am you, and you are me!"

His words drilled into my head every time he spoke. It's like he was shouting directly into my ears. Even a whisper would sound uncomfortably close.

"Why did you tie yourself?" he asked of me. He slurred his words when he spoke. His shattered face was completely clean, as if he had wiped himself off with a wet cloth. An audible second voice lagged behind his every word when he spoke. Sometimes it was feminine, sometimes it was a bit deeper or higher.

"You are not a saint," I said, breaking the silence on my end, "and you will never be."

He slammed his fists into the table, sending thunder through the room. I was startled, keeping my breath for a moment too long. The only recognisable part of his face, his eyebrows, rose. He breathed quickly, gasping for air weakly. An orange shimmer under his eye reflected the lantern's dying fire back to me. A tear.

"You've killed people."

I didn't know who said it. I didn't know where it came from.

"We haven't killed anyone," I pointed out. "I haven't."

He looked at me. His jaw dropped from one side, barely holding onto ligaments. Then, he took both of his hands and pressed his mandible back into his skull, spreading his fingers out over all pieces of a shattered plate. Finally, he smiled with his mouth back in place. Then a cough, subsequented by a laugh.

A shiver crawled down my spine. His laugh only grew with intensity and volume. Briefly, it muted the second voice's screams.

"You're nothing without me."

I blinked, and the light was out. He was gone. Instinctively I looked at my rope-burned wrists, noticing I had been freed. Or been free. I scraped my chair away from the table, and took a look around the room. His whispers circled inside my head, but I couldn't understand anything beyond the wind-like hiss of too many voices speaking at once.

I limped towards my front door, having forgotten how to use my legs. Passing by the mirror, I chose to ignore the unhealthy glow emitting from behind my right eye. I took my eye patch and slid it onto my face. The old charred leather gave me the sensation of wearing boots on my face. The flimsy work of cotton I had attached didn't help me, but it was all I had.

// SCENE 3

My boots sunk in mud with every step. Not being apart of Vertia officially meant going without the grave of gravel paths. I passed someone's camp on my way. The empty one I spotted every time, the one with a yet-smoking campfire. I tried not to step into any odd holes or traps left for rabbits. Before I had even reached the town, I could hear folksongs and the crackling of an uncannily tall celebratory fire.

The square was more crowded than usual. Merchants shouted over one another, children darted between boots, and the bonfire threw sparks into the evening sky.

I made straight for the bounty board. Fresh parchment drew a rare crowd, myself included. One sheet, still crisp at the corners, had another lingering. I pushed closer to see what had them staring.

It was a young woman with some hunting equipment. A dagger, a belt with a compass, and a pocket watch. As I got closer, I could've sworn I had felt their skin breathe a cold breeze at me. Then we stood side by side, reading it.

Having gotten ready to put it away, she stopped herself. "You interested?" she asked me, her voice seeming familiar.

A bounty hunter, I had realized. I shook my head, and she wrapped it up tightly and put it inside her satchel. For a moment she shifted her body away from the board, though she hesitated. She took a look at me, and not long after it had turned into a stare.

"I hope you know your patch doesn't hide whatever you're trying to hide," her freezing breath stung my neck.

I clenched my jaw, readjusting it over my eye. My heart palpitated, though she was gone before I could react. What an odd woman.

I watched her disappear into the crowd. The satchel bounced against her hip with every step until the sea of shoulders swallowed her whole. She had looked at me as if she'd recognised something she wished she hadn't.

My feet moved before I had decided to follow her. I squeezed between townsfolk, earning a few curses as I pushed through them. She was quick. Much quicker than someone carrying half a workshop on their belt had any right to be.

I spotted the compass hanging from her hip again just as she turned into a narrower street between two stone buildings. By the time I reached the corner, she'd already stopped. She didn't look back. One hand rested on the hilt of the dagger at her waist while the other adjusted the satchel over her shoulder. "You've followed me long enough," she said. "If you're after the bounty, find your own lead."

"I'm not."

"Then what?"

The question caught me off guard. I hadn't thought that far. I'd followed instinct more than reason. My eyes lingered on the pale shimmer beneath hers, subtle enough that I could have mistaken it for reflected daylight if I hadn't seen the same impossible glow in my own mirror every morning.

"Your eyes," I said before I could stop myself.

She exhaled through her nose. "Seriously?" She raised her hand and swatted at the air in front of me, shooting a surge of cold into my chest. My head got dizzy, almost falling off of my shoulders. I coughed with difficulty, feeling as if my lungs had been pierced. Tears had escaped my eyes as the winter wind hit my face. When I had reopened my eyes, she was gone. I stood there for a while, staring at the empty end of the alley. A few moments ago there had been someone standing there. Now there was only myself. My breathing gradually steadied, though the ache in my chest remained until I warmed up.

My fingers tightened around the edge of the patch without pulling it off. Whatever she had seen wasn’t something I could interpret, and it wasn’t something I was going to find answers for in a town like this. If she didn't care to give me a moment of her precious time, no one would. I took a deep breath before deciding to rip it off and leave it in the alley. If she didn't have to hide, I didn't either.

The noise around me dulled as I split my head thinking. There was no place in the world that would accept me. There was no place in the world that would interest me now, other than the cave. I didn't have a home, I didn't have a family, and I didn't have a purpose. Without thinking, I drew my hand and hit the brick wall as if my intention was to put a hole into it.

My eyes swelled up with tears. I was not special like her. The pain had hit me like thunder, and for a moment I swayed back and forth, until my breath left my body, and my eyes fell shut.

// SCENE 4

I saw it clearly in front of my eyes this time. Gasping, I made my way to the crystal, crossing the last few steps. Standing in front of it, I felt a sensation I hadn't felt in a while. It wasn't happiness, nor pride, nor satisfaction, but something between all three of them and soul-crushing anxiety. I reached my hand out. It felt warm to the touch. Not just the warmth of a dead campfire or a candle, but human warmth. I clasped my hand around its upward pointing sharp tip. Unlike the stalactites and the ice, the sides were completely smooth, like they had been sanded. I felt the pressure in my head release; I could breathe again. A melancholic ringing followed, precedenting his usual way of arrival. He wore the same scars I had given him earlier, blood spilling from rips in his skin.

He took a look at my hand, hovering over the crystal. A subtle smirk followed, immediately shifting into a concerned face. "What is this?" he asked me. The light in his eyes pulsed bright and dark, showing me glimpses of a reflection a split second at a time. I saw myself laugh, and cry, and for a moment even beg. In a way, I felt calm. Despite him being there with me, I felt all the tranquility in the world. A loud, energising tranquility. The crystal had to have been what I was looking for.

I shook my head. "You tell me," I spoke, hearing my voice echo, though inside my head this time. The moment the words left my mouth, the crystal pulsed once, not like a light, but a heartbeat. The sound didn’t echo; it duplicated, arriving from slightly different positions in space, as if the cave had forgotten its place. His expression tightened, then softened, then failed to hold either state long enough to mean anything. I felt my grip shift, closing around the crystal. My reflection appeared inside. Not mirrored, but earlier. A version of me standing still while I was already moving. The voice behind me stopped trying to speak like a person and started sounding like interference, like meaning being stripped out of language. I tried to let go of the crystal, but its beauty captivated me.

I looked at him and realized he was no longer standing in a consistent place. Every blink placed him a fraction closer, then farther, then split between both positions at once. The wounds I had given him earlier were gone in places and over-defined in others, as if the idea of damage was being edited rather than healed. The realization didn’t arrive as insight. It arrived as absence. A missing piece of structure I hadn’t noticed until it stopped supporting anything. The cave was not resisting me. It was arranging me. Every time I had returned here, every time the cold had “reset,” the space had been more precise, less accidental. Even him. His voice, his face, the way he insisted on being there, none of it reacted like opposition. It reacted like instruction.

My face contorted as I realised his intention. I wasn't here by chance. The hypnotic light he had led me to was going to swallow me whole, let me become one with him. I sighed audibly. "I'm done listening to you."

As my weight shifted preparing for a strike, his smile fell. Eyes wide, he screamed at me. To stop, to reconsider. His usual lies I would not listen to any longer. I wound up my fragile human hand, and let it shatter the crystal.

There was no impact sound at first. Only pressure. Then everything collapsed into a single, thin fracture of light that split the world open from the inside. The cave, the cold, the voice, the timing of my breath. Each of them were dismissed at once.

For the first time since I'd met him, he looked afraid.

"Kane."

His voice disappeared with everything else. It wasn't cut short so much as erased, as though the sound had never found the air in the first place. The fracture raced through the crystal beneath my fist, branching into a web of impossible lines that refused to settle into any shape I could follow. For a brief, impossible moment I saw the cave layered upon itself a hundred different ways. I saw the path behind me continue where I knew it had ended. I saw my own footprints leading away from the crystal before I had taken them. I saw him standing untouched beside the shattered remains while, at the same time, I saw him collapse to his knees clutching at his skull. The cave cried in pain, then it stopped. And then it laughed.

His terrified expression settled into an amused one. Then, slowly, impossibly, the corners of his mouth began to rise. It wasn't the arrogant grin he wore whenever he thought he'd cornered me. It wasn't mockery. It was relief. The kind of relief a drowning man wears when he finally breaks the surface. My stomach tightened. I took an instinctive step backwards, but I couldn't get away. The wounds I'd carved into his face sealed themselves without leaving so much as a scar, his posture straightened, and for the first time since I'd known him, there wasn't a trace of desperation left in his eyes. There was only certainty.

// SCENE 5

I woke up back in the alley.

My hand hurt immediately, before I was even fully aware of where I was. Beneath it all, there was a second pulse running through my body, misaligned with my heartbeat. And it didn’t change.
